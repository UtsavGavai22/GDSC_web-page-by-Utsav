What is GDSC?

For the last many years, India has been the birthplace of some of the finest tech talent in the world. In the last 5 years, digitisation in India has grown dramatically, a phenomenon that accelerated further as the pandemic forced people into homes and onto the internet. As an industry, and indeed, the country, we have a greater need than ever before for a pipeline of agile, brilliant young minds to help write the next chapter of India’s digitisation journey. The Google Developer Student Clubs (GDSC) program aims to empower student developers in universities to impact their student communities through technology. The program helps students bridge the gap between theory and practice in understanding the intersections between social groups, technology and user behaviour. Today, we are pleased to announce that applications for GDSC leads for the upcoming 2022-2023 academic year are now open.

Referrals, Graduation, and Post-GDSC Opportunities

The current leads are given referral codes that can be shared with aspiring students who wish to apply for leading a GDSC at their university before the applications for selecting new leads are opened. These codes are provided to the current leads a week prior to the public application release and can be shared with students from any institute, regardless of their qualifications. Keep in mind that having a referral doesn’t ensure that you will be selected or shortlisted for an interview, but it does offer some extra consideration to your application in the large pile of submissions that are made every year.

Craft that Perfect Application

Google accepts applications from graduate and under-graduate students via the Advocu platform. Applications for the Indian region are usually open in the final week of March and typically lasts over the next two to three weeks. The applications are evaluated on a rolling basis, so it’s advisable to apply as soon as possible. This can greatly enhance your chances of being selected for an interview. In my case, I applied within the first three days of the start of the application. You can also express your interest by filling out this form.Usually, as part of the application, you’ll be required to submit your personal details, answers to a few essay questions, and a 90-sec video. While applying, make sure that you strictly follow the guidelines regarding the nature and length of your answers. Your resume isn’t required when applying for GDSC

Interview with a Google Representative

Interviews, especially online ones, can be stressful. I was filled with anxiety on the morning of my interview as I had no prior knowledge of what to expect. My college never had a GDSC club and it was my first-ever interview To calm my nerves, I logged into the meeting somewhat 10 mins before the start of the interview. At exactly 12 PM, my interviewer joined the meet call but had to delay it by half an hour as his laptop needed charging. We then connected at around 12:20 PM, and the interview began with a friendly intro.
